3. Course Overview

This qualification provides individuals with the skills and knowledge to select, install, set up, test, fault find, repair and maintain electrical systems and equipment in buildings and premises.

It includes Electrical Regulatory Authority Council (ERAC), or their successor’s, Essential Performance Capabilities for an ‘Unrestricted Electrician’s license’.

The skills and knowledge described in this qualification require a licence or permit to practice in the workplace where work is carried out on electrical installations which are designed to operate at voltages greater than 50 volt (V) alternating current (a.c.) or 120 V direct current (d.c.).

Competency development activities in this qualification are subject to regulations directly related to licensing. Where a licence or permit to practice is not held, a relevant contract of training through an Australian Apprenticeship, may be required. To obtain an Unrestricted Electrician’s Licence in most jurisdictions the qualification must be completed as an apprenticeship or Trades Recognition Australia (TRA) pathway. Where required for Licencing, the certification documentation issued must indicate if the qualification was completed as an apprenticeship or Trades Recognition Australia (TRA) pathway.

    12. Theory Assessment conditions:

  • Your screen must be shared at all times.
  • Your camera must remain on so the supervisor can see you throughout the test.
  • The camera must be positioned so the supervisor can clearly see your face, hands, and workspace at all times.
  • Your microphone must remain on so the supervisor can hear if you speak.
  • You must be in a quiet room by yourself during the assessment. No other person is allowed in the room.
  • No notes, phones, or other materials may be used unless authorised by the trainer. A calculator is permitted.
  • At the beginning of the session, you may be asked to briefly show your surroundings using your camera.
  • Suspicious activity: Energy Space has a “Suspicious Activity” feature. If you open another tab or access unauthorised materials, the system will detect it, and the assessment may be stopped and marked as not satisfactory. This will count as an attempt, and you will need to reschedule.
  • Students are allowed two attempts per assessment (one resit). Additional attempts may incur fees. Please refer to your Letter of Offer and Student Handbook for details. Failure to follow these conditions may result in the assessment being stopped, rescheduled, or marked as invalid.

    16. Portfolio Documentation - End of qualification

    The End of Qualification Documentation is the final and essential stage of your qualification process.

    At this stage, you are required to submit a complete set of documents to verify your work experience, licensing status, and compliance with regulatory requirements.

    This documentation allows us to assess your eligibility for completion and certification.

    Please ensure that all forms are completed accurately and that all supporting evidence is current and valid

    All submitted documentation must be current and validated within the last three (3) months. Documents older than this period may not be accepted and may require re-issue or re-verification.

    Document Requirement Details
    Provisional Electrical Licence Must be valid
    Overseas Employer Reference Evidence of electrical work experience
    Australian Employer Reference(s) Minimum of 12 months experience
    Reference letter Reference letters must include:
    • Company letterhead/logo and contact details
    • Employer name, signature, and license number
    • Duration of employment, role, and industry activities
    • Detailed scope of electrical work undertaken

    Live LV Panel Rescue Certificate UETDRRF004 – issued within the last 12 months

    Referee Testimonials (x2) Form attached - From licensed electricians in Australia (only)
